SONDE

Spatial Oracle Network for Decentralized Environments

The Problem: Your phone constantly generates valuable data (location, WiFi signals, cell towers, network quality). Tech companies collect it, sell it for billions. You get $0.

The Solution: Install SONDE. Earn $5-25/month in crypto. Same data. You get paid.

1. What We Do

SONDE is a mobile app for Solana Seeker devices that collects anonymized sensor data in the background. Users earn cryptocurrency tokens. We sell the data to enterprises. Simple.

Seven Data Streams

Data Type What We Collect Privacy Protection
Cell Signal RSSI, RSRP, RSRQ, network type No PII, just signal quality
WiFi Network density, signal strength SSIDs hashed (one-way)
Bluetooth Device count only No MAC addresses stored
GPS Approximate location Fuzzed to ~100m radius
Motion Walking, driving, stationary No tracking, just state
Barometer Atmospheric pressure Anonymous
Network Speed Latency, bandwidth No browsing history

Collection frequency: Every 5 minutes, automatic.
Battery impact: Less than 2% per day.
Data usage: Approximately 10MB per day.

Hardware Attestation (TEEPIN)

Seeker devices have a secure enclave that cryptographically signs each data submission. This makes the data unfakeable. Enterprises pay 3-10x more for attested data because they can trust it. This is our moat.

2. Where The Money Comes From

We sell data to five customer segments. This is not theoretical—these companies already buy this data from competitors, often at higher prices for worse quality.

Customer Segment 1: Telecommunications ($50K-500K/month)

Buyers: Verizon, T-Mobile, AT&T, American Tower, Crown Castle, tower companies.

What they buy: Cell signal quality data—RSSI, RSRP, RSRQ, signal-to-noise ratio, network type (5G/LTE/4G), coverage maps.

Why they need it:

  • Network optimization: Identify dead zones, weak coverage areas, capacity constraints
  • 5G rollout planning: Determine where to install new towers
  • Regulatory compliance: FCC requires coverage reporting
  • Competitive benchmarking: Compare against competitors

Current alternatives: Telcos hire contractors to drive around cities with specialized equipment ("drive testing"). Cost: $50,000-$100,000 per city. Done once per year. SONDE provides continuous, real-time data at a fraction of the cost.

Precedent: OpenSignal built exactly this model. Crowdsourced cell signal data from 10+ million users. Acquired by Ookla (Speedtest.net) for $100+ million in 2018. OpenSignal didn't pay users anything. We do.

Customer Segment 2: Internet Service Providers ($20K-200K/month)

Buyers: Comcast, Spectrum, Verizon FiOS, municipal broadband projects, wireless ISPs.

What they buy: WiFi density maps, internet speed measurements (latency, bandwidth, jitter), coverage quality.

Why they need it:

  • Service expansion: Where should we build out fiber or add WiFi hotspots?
  • Quality monitoring: Are customers getting advertised speeds?
  • Competitive intelligence: How does our performance compare?
  • Customer retention: Identify areas with poor service before customers churn

Current alternatives: Speedtest by Ookla, customer surveys, FCC broadband maps (notoriously inaccurate). ISPs spend millions on network analytics.

Customer Segment 3: Transportation & Logistics ($10K-100K/month)

Buyers: Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, logistics companies, navigation providers (Google Maps, Waze, Apple Maps).

What they buy: Movement patterns, traffic density, motion state (walking/driving/stationary), route optimization data.

Why they need it:

  • Real-time traffic: Accurate congestion data for routing
  • ETA prediction: Better arrival time estimates
  • Demand forecasting: Where will riders need pickups?
  • Infrastructure planning: Cities use this for road improvements

Precedent: Google Maps generates $5+ billion per year, primarily from location data. Waze (acquired for $1.3 billion) was built on crowdsourced traffic data. The market is proven.

Customer Segment 4: Retail & Marketing ($5K-50K/month)

Buyers: Retail chains, shopping malls, marketing agencies, foot traffic analytics firms (SafeGraph, Foursquare, PlaceIQ).

What they buy: Foot traffic counts (Bluetooth proximity), dwell time, visit frequency, customer flow patterns.

Why they need it:

  • Store performance: Which locations get the most foot traffic?
  • Marketing attribution: Did our ad campaign drive store visits?
  • Competitive analysis: Is the competitor across the street busier?
  • Real estate decisions: Should we renew this lease?

Market size: Location intelligence market is $15+ billion annually. Companies like SafeGraph and Foursquare raised hundreds of millions to sell exactly this type of data.

Customer Segment 5: Weather & Climate ($5K-30K/month)

Buyers: Weather.com, AccuWeather, NOAA, climate research institutions, insurance companies.

What they buy: Barometric pressure readings, temperature, atmospheric data at hyperlocal resolution.

Why they need it:

  • Hyperlocal forecasts: City-level accuracy requires dense sensor networks
  • Storm tracking: Real-time pressure changes predict weather events
  • Climate research: Long-term atmospheric trends
  • Insurance: Risk assessment for property policies

Precedent: Weather Underground (acquired by IBM) built a network of home weather stations. Crowdsourced weather data is valuable and proven.

3. Why Enterprises Will Pay

Reason 1: We Have Hardware Attestation

Every data point from a Seeker device is cryptographically signed by a secure enclave (TEEPIN). This means:

  • Data cannot be spoofed or faked
  • No GPS spoofing, no emulators, no bots
  • Regulatory-grade quality (FCC, etc.)
  • Worth 3-10x premium pricing

Competitors cannot replicate this without access to Seeker hardware. This is our defensible advantage.

Reason 2: Cost-Effective Alternative

Current enterprise costs:

  • Telco drive testing: $50K-100K per city, once per year
  • Data brokers: $20K-200K/month for inferior data
  • In-house collection: Millions in infrastructure and personnel

SONDE provides better data, real-time, at lower cost.

Reason 3: Coverage & Density

150,000+ Solana Seeker devices already sold. Users carry phones everywhere: homes, offices, highways, rural areas, international travel. Fixed sensors can't compete with this coverage.

Reason 4: They Already Pay For Worse Data

This is not a new market. Enterprises currently buy:

  • Location data from data brokers
  • Speedtest data from Ookla
  • Foot traffic from SafeGraph/Foursquare
  • Weather data from Weather Underground

SONDE combines all of these, adds hardware attestation, and costs less. Switching is obvious.

8. Risks & Mitigation

Risk: Low User Adoption

Mitigation: Aggressive early adopter incentives (5x rewards), marketing through 150K Seeker owner base, referral program, partnership with Solana Mobile team.

Risk: No Enterprise Customers

Mitigation: Free pilots reduce barrier to entry. Market is proven (OpenSignal, Google Maps). Sales rep with industry connections. Flexible pricing.

Risk: Battery Drain Too High

Mitigation: Extensive optimization and testing. Target <2% per day. Allow users to customize collection frequency if needed.

Risk: Regulatory Issues

Mitigation: Privacy-first design (GDPR compliant from day one). Legal counsel engaged early. No PII collected. Decentralized governance (DAO) reduces regulatory surface area.

Risk: Token Price Collapse

Mitigation: Dual revenue model (emissions + enterprise revenue). Treasury buybacks. Burn mechanisms. Staking locks to reduce sell pressure.
